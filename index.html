WHAT ARE WE CREATING
TO MAKE A BETTER FUTURE?

Born and raised in the small town of Terryville, Connecticut (the birthplace of mass-production), I remember spending my childhood with the seasons: biking in the spring breeze, swimming in the summer sun, hiking in the autumn woods, and sledding on snowy winter days. Now that I think about it, that's also how I remember spending my adulthood.

Like many kids of the 1990s, I was born amidst the greatest technological advancements in human history. We grew up with the the speed of our modems. We shared birthdays with iPods. We knew how to Google before our parents did. We were raised with these accomplishments. Now, as adults, we are the ones responsible for the future.

MY DESIGN PROCESS

Planning

The most crucial phase. Get this wrong and it will feel like your whole world is falling apart. Lay out day by day, hour by hour, and add some padding to subtract any stress. Accepting the right balance of ambition is the key here. Plan for the minimum viable product, and then branch out the "WOW" features from there.

Execution

Grit your teeth and enjoy the ride. This is the most rewarding part of the project. Success is sweet and failure is a learning opportunity. Staying persistent is the key here. Before you know it you will be taking everything for granted and forget all about the troubles with your functional new app.

Polishing

You did it, but anybody could have. Time to pull out all the stops and make your application unforgettable. This is where creativity is key and you are free to show people something they could have never imagined. The beautiful harmony of art, mathematics, and technology.

SOME OF MY LATEST WORKS

RSS Blur

A full-stack application based on NewsBlur where users can follow their favorite RSS feeds. It is implemented on a single page using PostgreSQL, React.js, and AJAX requests to real-world feeds.


Bounty Hunter

A Javascript on HTML canvas derivation of the arcade classic Asteroids. With WASD + Spacebar commands handled by the Keymaster plugin, the player has to stay out of bankruptcy for as long as possible by collecting woolong bounties in space.

Pomo D'Oro

Based on the popular Pomodoro Technique, Pomo D'Oro is an AngularJS single-page application with a Rails on PostgreSQL backend which can be used to plan and manage tasks that bring a little bit of discord into the world.
